# AI Landing Zone Patterns

AI Landing Zones provide enterprise-scale, production-ready reference architectures for deploying secure and resilient AI applications and agents on Azure. This documentation covers the two primary implementation patterns and helps you choose the right approach for your organization.

## What is an AI Landing Zone?

An AI Landing Zone is an **application landing zone** that acts as a foundation for various AI workloads. It can be deployed with or without an existing platform landing zone, providing a secure, governed environment for AI applications.

<Note>
The AI Landing Zone focuses on [AI on Azure Platform (PaaS)](https://learn.microsoft.com/azure/cloud-adoption-framework/scenarios/ai/platform/architectures) and is designed per the [Cloud Adoption Framework AI Scenario](https://learn.microsoft.com/azure/cloud-adoption-framework/scenarios/ai/).
</Note>

## When to Use Each Pattern

### Choose APIM-Based When

- **Enterprise governance** is critical (regulated industries, multi-team environments)
- **Cost attribution** across business units is required
- **Token rate limiting** and quota management are needed
- **Multi-model routing** (Azure OpenAI, Foundry, third-party LLMs)
- **Centralized observability** and audit trails are mandatory
- **Hybrid or multi-cloud** AI scenarios

### Choose Foundry-Only When

- **Simpler deployment** is preferred
- **Team-level AI projects** without enterprise-wide governance needs
- **Built-in AI capabilities** are sufficient
- **Lower operational overhead** is a priority
- **Proof of concepts** or experimental workloads

## Relationship to Azure Landing Zones

The AI Landing Zone patterns integrate with [Azure Landing Zones](https://learn.microsoft.com/azure/cloud-adoption-framework/ready/landing-zone/) in two ways:

### With Existing Azure Landing Zone

The AI Landing Zone acts as a **supplemental application landing zone** that extends your existing platform landing zone:

- Reuses shared infrastructure (identity, networking, management)
- Follows enterprise governance policies
- Integrates with existing monitoring and logging
- Maintains security standards

### Standalone Deployment

For organizations without an existing Azure Landing Zone:

- Provides a complete foundation for AI workloads
- Includes necessary networking and governance components
- Can be integrated with a platform landing zone later

## Architecture Principles

All AI Landing Zone patterns follow these core principles:

1. **Separation of Concerns**: Central governance layer handles security and observability, while spokes focus on AI workload development
2. **Unified Oversight**: Single control plane provides visibility across all AI consumption
3. **Local Freedom**: Business units maintain autonomy within centrally-defined guardrails
4. **Defense in Depth**: Layered security with network isolation and centralized policy enforcement

## Prerequisites

Before implementing any AI Landing Zone pattern:

- Azure subscription with appropriate permissions
- Understanding of your organization's network topology
- Identity management strategy (Entra ID integration)
- Governance requirements and compliance frameworks
- Cost management and budget allocation processes
