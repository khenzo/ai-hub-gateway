# Sales Lead Engagement

The **Sales Lead Engagement** use case deploys intelligent agents that research prospects, identify warm paths through existing relationships, and draft personalized outreach—before a seller ever picks up the phone. When built on Citadel, these agents operate with enterprise-grade governance, identity management, and data protection across every customer interaction.

## The Challenge

Sellers spend hours researching prospects before outreach, or worse, they skip the research and send generic messages that get ignored. The information they need exists in CRM, news feeds, and relationship data across the organization—but assembling it for each prospect is manual and slow.

The impact compounds across the sales organization:

- **Wasted selling time** on manual research instead of customer conversations
- **Generic outreach** that fails to cut through noise and earn responses
- **Missed warm paths** because sellers don't know who in their organization already has a relationship
- **Lost deals** to competitors who personalize effectively and win the first conversation

For many B2B organizations, the bottleneck isn't a lack of data. It's the friction between scattered signals and the concise, actionable brief a seller needs before reaching out.

## The Opportunity

Deploy agents that research prospects automatically, identify warm paths through existing relationships, and draft personalized outreach grounded in real context. Before a seller ever reaches out, the agent has assembled a briefing: company news, relevant triggers, mutual connections, and talking points tailored to that prospect's situation.

The business outcomes are immediate:

- **Sellers engage with confidence** and spend less time on pre-call research
- **Response rates climb** when outreach is personalized and timely
- **Time to first conversation drops** as warm paths replace cold outreach
- **Revenue operations teams can own the solution** without requiring dedicated developer resources

## IQ Layer Mapping

Sales Lead Engagement agents draw on multiple intelligence layers to deliver contextual, personalized briefings:

- **Work IQ** provides relationship signals that reveal who in your organization has met with someone at the prospect company. This enables the agent to surface warm introductions, shared meeting history, and internal champions that would otherwise be invisible to the seller.
- **Fabric IQ** matches prospects against ideal customer profiles and account data. It understands which accounts fit your target segments, their engagement history, and relevant business metrics that shape the outreach strategy.
- **Foundry IQ** enriches prospect briefings with company research, news, and triggers from public and proprietary sources. Rather than building separate connectors for each data feed, Foundry IQ provides reusable knowledge bases that keep briefings current and relevant.

## Citadel Placement

Deploying Sales Lead Engagement agents on Citadel ensures they meet enterprise security and governance requirements from day one. Each Citadel layer plays a specific role:

- **Layer 1 (Governance Hub / APIM)** routes all agent API calls to CRM and external data sources, enforcing rate limiting, authentication, and runtime policy. All Copilot Studio and Foundry agent API calls should pass through the Citadel Governance Hub to enable unified cost attribution and policy enforcement.
- **Layer 3 (Agent Identity / Agent 365)** manages agent identity, sponsorship, and lifecycle for every deployed prospecting agent. Register each agent with a unique identity and implement sponsorship models for agent permissions when accessing CRM and customer data.
- **Layer 4 (Security Fabric)** applies PII protection and data governance via Purview across all prospect data. Use Purview to govern access to Work IQ, Fabric IQ, and Foundry IQ data sources, and apply Entra conditional access policies to agent service principals handling customer information.

## Customer Example

A **B2B technology company** deployed a lead enrichment agent that runs nightly against new inbound leads. The agent identifies existing relationships via Work IQ, scores fit with the company's product using Fabric IQ, and pulls recent news and triggers via Foundry IQ.

Sellers receive a prioritized list each morning with context summaries and suggested talking points—all saved in CRM. Response rates on outbound increased double-digits, and sellers report spending far less time on pre-call research.

## Buying Pattern Fit

- **Primary audience:** Dynamics 365 Sales or Salesforce customers with a B2B sales motion
- **Key indicators:** Outbound or ABM strategy, large addressable market, or sellers struggling with research efficiency
- **Quick win:** Run a pilot for a single territory or segment and measure response rate improvement within 30 days
