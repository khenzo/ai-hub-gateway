## Overview

This page maps every requirement from the EY AI Agent Development Framework to its corresponding Citadel component or architecture layer. Use the enforcement status column to identify what Citadel covers, what requires supplemental process, and where to look for additional tooling.

### Documental Process Controls

The EY framework also includes three governance assessments that are **documental process steps** rather than technical infrastructure controls. They are listed here for completeness but are **not included in the alignment map** because they require EY-internal forms, manual review, and organisational sign-off rather than Azure platform configuration:

- **Responsible AI (RAI) Global Policy** — EY policy attestation and documentation requirement
- **Data Protection AI Assessment** — EY data protection review process
- **Information Security AI Assessment** — EY information security review process

Citadel provides the underlying technical guardrails ([Content Safety](/governance/content-safety), [PII blocking](/governance/pii-protection), Defender for AI) that support these assessments, but the assessments themselves remain EY process controls outside the scope of this technical alignment.

### Summary

<Info>
  **Enforced**: Citadel natively implements 14 of 24 mapped criteria through Azure infrastructure, APIM policies, or the 4-layer architecture — no additional tooling required.
</Info>

<Warning>
  **Partial**: 8 mapped criteria require Citadel plus an EY process step (AIRA, service-line approval) or a supplemental tool for full coverage.
</Warning>

<Note>
  **Attention Points**: 2 criteria fall outside Citadel's scope: NVIDIA stack and EY-proprietary tooling or internal forms. The three documental assessments (RAI, Data Protection, Information Security) are mentioned above but excluded from the technical alignment map.
</Note>
